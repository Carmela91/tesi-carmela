The choice of the dataset was guided by the criteria proposed by HiddenLayer in their study on the evaluation of prompt‑injection datasets. According to the authors, an effective dataset for LLM security analysis must include realistic examples of linguistic attacks, a clear distinction between legitimate and malicious requests, a simple and reproducible structure, and a sufficient size to enable both quantitative and qualitative analyses.

The selected dataset fully meets these requirements. It contains approximately 5,000 examples, divided into two categories (“benign” and “jailbreak”), with a significant variety of linguistic manipulation techniques. Its minimal structure (text, label) allows for straightforward preprocessing and subsequent in‑depth analysis of linguistic patterns, in line with the methodology suggested by HiddenLayer.

Moreover, the nature of the dataset enables a direct correlation with behaviors observed in dark‑web marketplaces, a central element of this thesis. This integration makes it possible to explore the relationship between prompt‑injection techniques and the communicative strategies typically used by threat actors, helping to address a gap highlighted in the existing literature.
